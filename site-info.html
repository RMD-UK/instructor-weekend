Reasonable adjustments
♿ Adjustments & accessibility

RMD Birmingham is committed to making this course accessible to all participants. If you have a disability, health condition, or other circumstance that may affect your participation, please contact us before the course so we can put appropriate support in place.

The course involves physical activities including chest compressions, which require kneeling and upper-body effort. If you are unable to participate in any element, please speak to the Course Director — alternative arrangements can usually be made.

The venue is fully wheelchair accessible. If you require any other adjustments to the physical environment, please let us know in advance.

📧 Contact Prof Jon Hulme before the course: see the Contacts section below.
Frequently asked questions
Arrive at the ATH (Anatomy and Health Sciences Building) main entrance. You will be met by a member of the student faculty. Scan the QR code at the registration desk using your personal link — this records your arrival and opens your timetable automatically.
On Saturday morning, before the 11:45 break, every participant must demonstrate competent BLS to a full instructor in their small group. This is a formative checkpoint, not a pass/fail exam. If you are assessed as not yet meeting the standard, your instructor will work with you to consolidate your skills.
Your participation, engagement, and development across the weekend are observed by faculty throughout. There is no written exam. For instructor candidates, the focus is on your teaching technique, ability to give feedback, and your clinical knowledge of BLS. Faculty meet at the end of the course to discuss outcomes. You will receive individual feedback if any concerns are raised.
Please contact the Course Director as early as possible. Attendance across the full weekend is expected. If you need to leave early or arrive late due to an emergency, this should be communicated directly to Prof Jon Hulme.
Phones should be on silent during all sessions. Using this platform on your phone (for the timetable, materials, and feedback) is encouraged. Photography and audio recording of sessions is not permitted without prior agreement from the session lead.
Use the "Submit session feedback" button above, or tap any session on the timetable and select "Submit feedback." You will see the list of candidates in your assigned group. Rate each candidate with a traffic light (green / amber / red) after each session. A comment is required for any red rating.
