How To Assess Plenary Lecture And Demonstration

The aim of this session is to allow candidates to observe the format of student assessments. The total session time (lecture + demonstration) is 35 minutes.

Pre-session requirements
  • This session needs more than one person (names in programme) to perform demonstration assessment:
  • Facilitator
  • Assessor (with phone as prop)
  • Assistant
  • Candidate (must be senior member of faculty)
  • The candidate will include small errors (e.g. chest compressions 28:2, 29:2, 32:2) but still perform at a standard to pass assessment
  • Facilitator must run through the How To Assess lecture prior to day.
  • All members of group must meet before session to practice.
  • A senior faculty member must be present to confirm demonstration is correct.
  • Ensure Zoom connectivity. A second laptop/mobile phone should be joined to the call to act as a ‘roving camera’ to capture the demonstration for the stream.
Running the session
Stage / RoleInstructions
PRE-SESSION CHECK Manikin and AED set upLungs inflate without problem Clicker turned off Blue pad underneath manikin to kneel on AED working and pads are sticky Confirm microphones work & zoom connectivity satisfactory.
FacilitatorIntroduce session Explain the importance to candidates Awareness of exam process at end of course To prepare their students for final assessment Assessment is part of teaching process Give lecture on how to assess Highlight important points and techniques Do not read out points but expand on them This is the lecture slides entitled ‘7 - assessing’ in the google drive The lecture should last 15 minutes
DemonstrationThe assessor sets up as they would for a real exam. As part of set: Check the manikin (clicker off) Briefs the assistant As part of dialogue: Call candidate in and introduce themselves Check identity; log candidate on phone Give the candidate a scenario Ask if any questions Get them to repeat the scenario back Assess the candidate “Freeze frame” (stop talking and moving as if time has stopped)
Facilitate Discussion With AudienceUse exam form to facilitate discussion about what the candidate did well and what they could improve on. Does audience they think the candidate should be passed or not? Once a clear decision has been made by the audience then return to the demonstration
DemonstrationExit “freeze frame” Give candidate result Record result on phone Candidate leaves
FacilitatorExplain what to say when passing a candidate or if they have not been successful i.e. succinct “You have passed” “I’m afraid you have not been successful in the assessment” Closure: Any questions and where candidates are going next. Summarise the session’s 3 most salient points
