Skills Teaching (Practical)

The aim of this session is to allow candidates to practice teaching students using the 3-stage technique and set-dialogue-closure structure with guidance.

Pre-session requirements
  • Arrange teaching space (chairs and space)
  • Set up and check manikin
  • Set up and check AED
  • Access to pen, paper and stopwatch
  • Access to ‘skill station - feedback form’ from RMD pack
  • Confirm which senior RMD member is going to act as your support for the session
  • There is 1hr 45 mins allocated for this session
Running the session
Stage / RoleInstructions
Manikin set up BY INSTRUCTOR BEFORE SESSIONLungs inflate without problem Clicker turned off Blue pad underneath manikin to kneel on
AED set up BY INSTRUCTOR BEFORE SESSIONTurns on (batteries OK) Pads present and sticky Correct scenario selected
IntroductionInform candidates of session aim Prepare, open, facilitate, close They will all teach a skill for 7 minutes and include all 3-stages (teach it, facilitate it, assess it) Split group into two
Divide teaching topics between candidates in groupTopics are Approach safely – shout help Open airway, check breathing, call 999 Chest compressions (30) Rescue breathing (2) Recovery position AED Mentoring bystander to do resuscitation Emphasise role of scenario setting To add detail to resuscitation Not over complicated Not unrealistic (must be reasonable) Some candidates may teach more than one topic or more than one person may teach the same topic. Both are acceptable. No topics are to be omitted.
Brief candidates about teachingUse ‘scenario and assessment feedback form’ from their RMD pack to plan their teaching They must use prepare, open, facilitate, close and include all three stages within facilitate (teach, facilitate and assess) Teaching will last 7-minutes (they will be stopped if necessary)
Group will rate the performance of the candidate acting as an instructorUse the ‘scenario and assessment feedback form’ from their RMD pack Make notes on it relating to performance Feedback consistently to candidate. No more than 3 minutes per person. You must use the following ‘Learning Conversation’ format: Make opening gambit. Jointly explore issues. Include suggestions from rest of group. Share thoughts using advocacy with inquiry. Check whether anyone else wants to discuss any further issues. Summarise.
Reinforce BLS/AED techniqueHighlight areas of good performance and those needing improvement (or common mistakes)
