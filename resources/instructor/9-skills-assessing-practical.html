Continuous Assessment (Practical)

The aim of this session is to allow candidates to practice assessing students performing BLS/AED and discuss appropriate standards for a passmark.

Pre-session requirements
  • Arrange teaching space (chairs and space)
  • Set up and check manikin
  • Set up and check AED
  • Access to pen, paper and stopwatch
  • Access to ‘BLS assessment record’ and ‘AED assessment record’ from RMD pack
  • Confirm which senior RMD member is going to act as your support for the session
Running the session
Stage / RoleInstructions
Manikin set up BY INSTRUCTOR BEFORE SESSIONLungs inflate without problem Clicker turned off Blue pad underneath manikin to kneel on
AED set up BY INSTRUCTOR BEFORE SESSIONTurns on (batteries OK) Pads present and sticky Correct scenario selected
IntroductionInform candidates of session aim - to practice the ‘assess it’ aspect of the three stage teaching approach They will all assess a life support skill and need to decide if they would pass the candidate
Nominate candidates in group as assessors (ie they will conduct assessment)½ will assess BLS, ½ will assess AED Emphasise role of scenario setting To add detail to resuscitation Not over complicated Not unrealistic Reasonable
Brief candidates acting as students (ie they will be assessed)For first BLS or AED scenario, tell them to do it perfectly (be aware – they probably won’t manage this but ask them to try) For subsequent scenarios, ask them to do some incorrectly. Examples include: CPR rate too slow / fast Not 30:2 No successful breaths Pads on wrong sides (i.e. left upper chest, right flank) For added complexity & decision making: CPR rate just within accepted limits (100-120) Ratio varies 29:2, 31:2, etc (this is acceptable) Most, but not all breaths, successful Pads inverted, but still in correct location
Brief candidates remaining in groupThey should watch candidate performance and decide if they should pass Use ‘BLS or AED assessment record’ from RMD pack as appropriate
Rate the performance of the candidate acting as an assessorUse the ‘scenario and assessment feedback form’ from their RMD pack Make notes on it relating to performance Feedback consistently to candidate. You must use the following format (the learning conversation): Make opening gambit. Jointly explore issues. Include suggestions from the rest of group. Share thoughts using advocacy with inquiry. Check whether anyone else wants to discuss any further issues. Summarise.
Dealing with difficult candidatesContinue practice BLS/AED teaching as before Brief candidates acting as students being assessed to portray potentially difficult character traits (see ERC booklet for full list) Facilitate discussion about how to deal with these situations during group teaching sessions
Instructor – conclusionBring the group back together. Explain that they have practiced assessment. Any questions about the session Explain where to go to next (break) Closure (Make sure these are the last things you say to the candidates) 3 points about the session drawing on the most important points for the group
Master copy of performanceRecord if performance satisfactory or not Enter this onto database on course laptop If not satisfactory, keep a copy of ‘scenario and assessment feedback form’ from their RMD pack with full details. Inform course director.
