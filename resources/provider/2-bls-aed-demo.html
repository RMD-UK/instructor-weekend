Recognition Of Heart Attack & Bls/Aed

The aim of this session is to describe common symptoms of a heart attack (a common cause for ​​ cardiac arrest) followed by a description and then demonstration of basic life support & automated external defibrillator skills

Running the session
Stage / RoleInstructions
Manikin and session set up (Instructor)Make sure manikin and AED working (do this during registration) Put manikin on the stage on blue mat before the scheduled start of the course introduction. Place the manikin longitudinal to audience (that is, head pointing to one side of stage & feet to the other) Put AED at the rear of the room for second instructor to retrieve during scenario Projector screen will be in use for the introductory talk: ensure that the ‘Recognition of heart attack’ and BLS slides display properly
Recognition of a heart attackReview slides in advance, and practice before the weekend starts Explain what is about to happen followed by descriptions & demonstrations of life support skills Relevance = heart attack is a common cause of cardiac arrest in the community Play video
Plenary lecture: BLS/AEDExplain that this is description of most up to date guidelines of life support skills that will be taught & tested on the rest of the course Present slide set Introduce facilitator for demonstration
Session introduction (facilitator)Explain what is about to happen: a real-time demonstration of management of the collapsed patient including use of BLS & AED skills
Real-time run throughInstructor 1 to demonstrate clearly BLS/AED on manikin Make sure clear movements Safety check: from a distance and in the vicinity of the patient Shake & shout Call loudly for help (patient is not responsive) 2 fingers under chin (thumb out of the way) Use actual timing (i.e. listen, look and feel for breaths for up to 10 seconds) 999 call (using mobile phone) & whilst on the phone, start chest compressions Care with hand placement: show one hand on the centre of the chest and then other hand placed on top of it Deliver best quality chest compressions Continue resuscitation 30:2 (give rescue breaths) When instructor 2 arrives ask them to fetch an AED Instructor 2: Do not arrive immediately, allow 30-60 seconds so there has been time for chest compressions & rescue breaths. You should act shocked: “Oh my goodness, what’s happened, is everything ok?” Once briefed by Instructor 1, say out loud you are going to get an AED. Leave the scenario, go to back of the room and count to 60 before returning with the AED Instructor 1 to demonstrate AED use: Turn on AED, and place on same side as you are kneeling Make sure look around before pressing shock on AED Make sure not looking at shock button but around manikin when pressing shock button Once shock delivered, resume CPR
BLS/AED Lecture (Facilitator)Review slides in advance, and practice before the weekend starts Make sure you emphasise: Correct order of the algorithm What to tell operator & numbers to dial Rate, depth & location of compressions Rescue breaths When to stop
Conclusions (Facilitator)Any questions answered Summary of session Where to go for practical work in small groups
EndRMD Course Team to clear up the manikin and AED and presentation
